Hyde Park

Saddle up and take a horseback ride in Hyde Park, Henry VII's favourite hunting ground.

Horseback riding in Hyde Park has long been a popular pastime, especially during the Victorian era, when upper-class Londoners would flock to the park to promenade or ride down the famous "Route du Roi" (King's Road), later delightfully called "Rotten Row".

There are two designated bridleways in the park, where horses transport visitors, members of the local stables, police, and even the Household Cavalry unit.

While hunting is no longer permitted, there are plenty of sights to see in Hyde Park. Look for the beautiful Serpentine Lake, the Diana Memorial Fountain, and - if it's a Sunday - Speaker's Corner, where passionate rabble-rousers can express themselves on whatever topic interests them.
